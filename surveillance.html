Surveillance Solutions

Surveillance security technologies are used in real-time monitoring and protection of people, property, and assets. They can be classified into three types: Wide and Medium area surveillance, which make use of cameras, radar, and satellite imagery to monitor large areas; small area surveillance, which uses cameras, sensors, and software to monitor small areas; and personal surveillance, which uses GPS tracking, wearable devices, and biometric identification to monitor individuals. These technologies are critical for a variety of applications, including border security, critical infrastructure protection, and disaster response, where they provide real-time situational awareness, detect potential threats, and aid in search and rescue operations. The integration of advanced software allows the data collected by these technologies to be evaluated in real-time, providing users with alerts and notifications from all the various surveillance equipment they have access.

    • MEDIUM AREA SURVEILLANCE
    Medium Area Surveillance (MAS) is a security device that detects and tracks humans, vehicles, and other things within a defined area using various technologies such as cameras, sensors, radar, and software. These technologies are incorporated into a complete security system that enables real-time monitoring and decision-making in order to identify, track, and respond to threats before they become serious. MAS systems are used to monitor and identify security threats at public events, airports, water treatment facilities, and power plants, among other places, and have a wide range of applications, including border security, critical infrastructure protection, and event security.

    • SMALL AREA SURVEILLANCE
    Small Area Surveillance (SAS) is a crucial technology used in security systems to monitor and secure specific areas. SAS systems use cameras, sensors, and software to capture visual data, detect motion, sound, and other environmental changes, and integrate the data into a single system. The software analyses the data and provides alerts and notifications to security personnel, and can also generate reports and visualise data in real-time. SAS systems are used in building security, retail security, and traffic monitoring to provide real-time situational awareness and detect potential security threats.
